How Freemasonry Started

Modern Freemasonry is generally traced to the early 18th century. In 1717, four London lodges formed the first Grand Lodge, creating a formal structure for organized modern Freemasonry.

The movement drew inspiration from medieval stonemason traditions, but developed into a fraternity focused on moral philosophy, friendship, charity and personal improvement.

Who Started Modern Freemasonry?

The Four London Lodges

In 1717, four London lodges joined together to create the first Grand Lodge of England. This event is widely associated with the beginning of organized modern Freemasonry.

Early Leaders

Early figures such as John Theophilus Desaguliers contributed to the development of Masonic writings, organization and public structure.

Historical Roots

Freemasonry used traditions from medieval craft guilds as symbols for personal growth, discipline and moral improvement.

How Freemasonry Spread

18th Century

Lodges expanded across Europe and North America through travel, trade and social connections.

19th Century

Freemasonry reached parts of Africa, Asia and other regions through international communities and historical migration.

Modern Era

Today, Masonic organizations operate in many countries under different recognized jurisdictions.

How Freemasonry Reached Kenya

Freemasonry reached Kenya during the colonial period through British administrators, railway workers, merchants, professionals and other international communities living in East Africa.

As towns such as Nairobi and Mombasa developed, organized lodges were established within different Masonic jurisdictions.
